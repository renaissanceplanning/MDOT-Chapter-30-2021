Coding Assumptions

The analysis of Project 22-10 required developing General Transit Feed Specification (GTFS) files to model the proposed fixed route bus service.

Project 22-10 is comprised of two new fixed bus routes.

  1. Service between Annapolis and Arundel Mills would carry passengers along a 29-mile route between. Fixed route bus service would operate with terminal stations at Annapolis Mall (Bestgate Road & Mall Entrance) and Arundel Mills Mall (Arundel Mills Circle). Three intermediate stops would be served: one at Cromwell Light Rail station and two at BWI Airport (where route 75 currently stops at terminals). Service would be provided weekdays from 6:00 AM to 7:00 PM, with Saturday service operating between from 8:00 AM to 7:00 PM. A 30-minute headway would be maintained during peak hours. A 60-minute headway would be operated at other times of the day.
  2. Service between Annapolis and Crofton would operate along a 12-mile route, with terminal stations at Annapolis Mall (Bestgate Road & Mall Entrance) and Crofton Country Club (Crofton Parkway & Crawford Boulevard). The fixed route bus service would serve four intermediate stops: Riva Road & Town Center Drive, Forest Drive & Riva Road, Forest Drive & Harker Place, and MD 2 & Somerville Road. Service would be provided weekdays from 6:00 AM to 7:00 PM, with weekend and holidays service operating between from 8:00 AM to 7:00 PM. A 60-minute headway would be maintained at all times and days.

Attributes

The attributes of the base multimodal network were modified through the addition of the newly developed GTFS feed modeling Project 22-10. The details of how Project 22-10 was represented in GTFS are provided below by describing four key tables of the feeds: Stops.txt, Trips.txt, Stop_times.txt, and Frequencies.txt.

Other tables in the new project feed are based on standard tables defined in the Chapter 30 Transit Accessibility Scoring Guide (“Standard GTFS Tables” section) and are not described here.

Trips.txt (vehicle-trip enumeration)

The trips file represents a single route with a seven-day travel profile. The scoring methodology is based around peak AM travel times. The feed was only developed to represent this time period and the associated frequencies and characteristics.

Table 2. Trips Table

Stop_times.txt (transit schedule)

The stop times table was developed by determining an average travel speed based on the travel time and approximate length of the line.

Table 3. Stops Times Table

Frequencies.txt (frequency of recurring trips)

A frequencies table was developed to model the headways of the line during weekday AM peak travel times. The table defines a start and end time for a headway, with each headway associated with a trip ID. The headways are provided in seconds. The trip IDs link the headway times to the individual trips that comprise the route described in the trips.txt file. When frequencies table is used, the stop times file defines a template of stop sequences and travel times between stops, and the frequencies table defines the interval of recurrence for each trip following the stop times template.
